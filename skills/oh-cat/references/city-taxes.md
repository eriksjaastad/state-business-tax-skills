# Ohio City/Municipal Business Tax Overlays

Last updated: 2026-04-11

Ohio cities impose **municipal income taxes** on business net profits. These are net income taxes — they tax business profits after expenses, not gross receipts. Municipal income taxes are entirely separate from the state CAT.

Ohio has over 600 municipalities with income taxes, administered either by the city directly or through the Regional Income Tax Agency (RITA) or Central Collection Agency (CCA).

Cities are listed by population (largest first).

---

## Columbus (Pop. ~900,000)

| Item | Detail | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Rate | 2.50% of net profit | [Columbus Income Tax Division](https://www.columbus.gov/Government/City-Auditor/Income-Tax-Division/General-Income-Tax-Information) |
| Who Must File | All businesses with activity in Columbus, even if a loss | [Columbus Income Tax Division](https://www.columbus.gov/Government/City-Auditor/Income-Tax-Division/General-Income-Tax-Information) |
| Filing Due Date | April 15 | [Columbus Income Tax Division](https://www.columbus.gov/Government/City-Auditor/Income-Tax-Division) |
| Filing URL | [columbus.gov/Government/City-Auditor/Income-Tax-Division](https://www.columbus.gov/Government/City-Auditor/Income-Tax-Division) | — |

**Note:** Net profits of all unincorporated businesses (sole proprietorships, partnerships, rentals) are taxable. Businesses must file even in loss years.
