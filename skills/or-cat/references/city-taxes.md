# Oregon City/County Business Tax Overlays

Last updated: 2026-04-11

Unlike Washington's city B&O taxes (gross receipts), Oregon's local business taxes are primarily **net income taxes**. Portland and Multnomah County are the only major Oregon jurisdictions with local business taxes. Salem and Eugene do not impose city-level business taxes.

---

## Portland (Pop. ~640,000)

Portland administers three local business taxes on a single combined return.

### Portland Business License Tax (BLT)

| Item | Detail | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Rate | 2.6% of net income | [Portland Revenue](https://www.portland.gov/revenue/business-tax) |
| Gross Receipts Exemption (2026+) | $75,000 (raised from $50,000) | [Portland Revenue](https://www.portland.gov/revenue/business-tax) |
| Owner Compensation Deduction (2025+) | $160,500 per owner | [Portland Form SP-2025 Instructions](https://www.portland.gov/revenue/documents/instructions-form-sp-2025/download) |
| Filing Due Date | April 15 (same as federal/state) | [Portland Revenue](https://www.portland.gov/revenue/business-tax) |
| Estimated Quarterly Payments | Required if $1,000+ liability (current and prior year) | [Portland Revenue](https://www.portland.gov/revenue/business-tax) |
| Filing URL | [pro.portland.gov](https://pro.portland.gov) | — |

### Multnomah County Business Income Tax (MCBIT)

| Item | Detail | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Rate | 2.0% of net income | [Multnomah County MCBIT](https://multco.us/info/multnomah-county-business-income-tax-mcbit) |
| Gross Receipts Exemption | $100,000 | [Multnomah County MCBIT](https://multco.us/info/multnomah-county-business-income-tax-mcbit) |
| Owner Compensation Deduction (2025+) | $158,500 per owner | [Portland Form SP-2025 Instructions](https://www.portland.gov/revenue/documents/instructions-form-sp-2025/download) |
| Filing Due Date | April 15 (filed with Portland BLT) | [Multnomah County MCBIT](https://multco.us/info/multnomah-county-business-income-tax-mcbit) |
| Filing URL | Filed on Portland combined return | — |

### Metro Supportive Housing Services (SHS) Business Tax

| Item | Detail | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Rate | 1.0% of net income | [Portland Revenue](https://www.portland.gov/revenue/business-tax) |
| Gross Receipts Threshold | $5,000,000+ (businesses with $5M+ gross receipts worldwide) | [Portland Revenue](https://www.portland.gov/revenue/business-tax) |
| Filing Due Date | April 15 (filed with Portland combined return) | [Portland Revenue](https://www.portland.gov/revenue/business-tax) |
| Filing URL | Filed on Portland combined return | — |

### Important Notes

- All three taxes are filed on the **same combined return** administered by the City of Portland[^1]
- These are **net income taxes**, not gross receipts taxes — business expenses reduce the tax base
- Businesses below the gross receipts exemption threshold may not owe tax but may still need to file
- Contact: biztaxhelp@portlandoregon.gov / 503-823-5157

[^1]: [Portland Business Tax Filing](https://www.portland.gov/revenue/business-tax)

---

## Salem (Pop. ~180,000)

Salem does not impose a city-level business income tax or gross receipts tax. Businesses operating in Salem are subject only to the state-level CAT and Oregon income tax.

---

## Eugene (Pop. ~175,000)

Eugene does not impose a city-level business income tax or gross receipts tax. Businesses operating in Eugene are subject only to the state-level CAT and Oregon income tax.
